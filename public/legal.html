Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 10, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how ChorEscape ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and mobile application (the "Service"). This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

Cookies allow websites to remember your preferences, improve functionality, and analyze website traffic. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies if you prefer.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Service to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. You cannot opt-out of essential cookies as the Service will not work without them.

  • Authentication: Remember your login status and session information
  • Security: Detect fraudulent activity and prevent security breaches
  • Load Balancing: Distribute traffic across servers to ensure optimal performance

Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Service by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve the Service's performance and user experience.

  • Analytics: Track pages visited, time spent on pages, and navigation patterns
  • Error Tracking: Identify and fix bugs and errors
  • Performance Monitoring: Monitor page load times and Service responsiveness

We use third-party analytics services, such as Google Analytics, which may set their own cookies to collect information about your use of the Service.

Functional Cookies

These cookies allow the Service to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features.

  • Preferences: Remember your language, region, and display preferences
  • User Settings: Store your account settings and preferences
  • Enhanced Features: Enable features like chat support and personalized content

Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to you and your interests. They also help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

  • Targeted Advertising: Display ads based on your browsing history and interests
  • Ad Performance: Measure how many people view and click on advertisements
  • Retargeting: Show you relevant ads on other websites after you visit our Service

Note: We only use advertising cookies with your explicit consent, where required by law. You can withdraw your consent at any time through your browser settings or our cookie preferences.

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics, deliver advertisements, and enhance the Service. These include:

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics and performance tracking
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment processing
  • Social Media Platforms: For social sharing features and login integration
  • Advertising Networks: For targeted advertising (with your consent)

Managing Cookies

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, limiting cookies may impact your ability to use the Service. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site permissions

Cookie Preferences

You can manage your cookie preferences through our cookie consent banner when you first visit our Service. You can update these preferences at any time by accessing the cookie settings in your account or through our cookie preferences page.

Opting Out

You can opt out of certain third-party cookies through the following resources:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Opt-out Page
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: DAA Opt-out Page

Note: Opting out does not mean you will stop seeing advertisements. It means the advertisements you see may be less relevant to your interests.

Mobile Devices

On mobile devices, you can manage cookies through your device settings. Please refer to your device manufacturer's instructions for specific guidance.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted. As a result, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or mechanisms.

Cookie Retention

Cookies have different lifespans:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them

The retention period for cookies varies depending on their purpose. Essential cookies are typically session cookies, while functional and performance cookies may persist for longer periods.
